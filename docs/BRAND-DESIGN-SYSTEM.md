## 1 · Brand Positioning

**Category we play in:** Strategic finance partnership for ambitious companies.
**Category we refuse:** Commodity bookkeeping / "the accountants."

### Positioning statement
> For founders and finance leaders who need their numbers to drive decisions — not just close the month — **ACCOUNTCLERKS** is the strategic finance partner that turns clean books into clear direction. Unlike traditional accounting firms that deliver compliance and disappear, we sit beside the business, translating the ledger into the next move.

### The reframe
| From (legacy accounting) | To (ACCOUNTCLERKS) |
| --- | --- |
| Records the past | Informs the next decision |
| Year-end deliverable | Always-on partnership |
| Compliance as the product | Compliance as the floor |
| "Here are your statements." | "Here's what they mean, and what to do." |
| Talks to your accountant | Sits in your strategy room |

### Positioning pillars
1. **Strategic, not clerical.** Every output ends in a recommendation, not a PDF.
2. **Precision you can trust blind.** Reconciled to the cent — that's the price of entry, not the pitch.
3. **Plain-spoken authority.** Boardroom rigor, founder-friendly language.
4. **A real team, amplified by software.** People who own outcomes; tooling that removes the busywork.

### Audience
- **Primary:** Founders & operators of $2M–$50M revenue companies (SaaS, agencies, e-commerce, healthcare, professional services).
- **Secondary:** Heads of Finance / fractional CFOs who need a dependable execution layer.
- **Buying triggers:** raising a round, scaling headcount, messy back-books, a board that wants real numbers, outgrowing a solo bookkeeper.

### One-line elevator
*"We're the finance team that closes your books and then tells you what to do about them."*

## 2 · Brand Story

Most companies meet their numbers too late. The books close weeks after the month they describe, land as a static report, and answer questions nobody is still asking. Finance becomes a rear-view mirror — accurate, and useless for steering.

ACCOUNTCLERKS was built on a simple conviction: **the ledger is the most honest story a company tells about itself** — and someone should be reading it out loud, in time to matter.

So we built a different kind of finance partner. Dedicated clerks who own your books end to end, working inside software that reconciles in real time, so the close is a status and not a scramble. But the close is where most firms stop and where we start. We read what the numbers are saying — about runway, margin, pricing, the hire you're about to make — and we say it plainly, every month, to the people making the call.

We named ourselves **ACCOUNTCLERKS** on purpose. The clerk is the original keeper of the record — meticulous, trusted, unglamorous, indispensable. We kept the discipline and dropped the dust. Today the clerk doesn't just keep the book. The clerk reads it, and helps you write the next chapter.

That's what we mean by **Beyond the Numbers.**

## 5 · Core Values

Five values. Each is a behavior, not a poster.

**1. Precision is the floor.**
Reconciled to the cent, every time. We don't celebrate accuracy — we assume it. It's the foundation everything else stands on.

**2. Say the real thing.**
We translate, we don't obscure. If the runway is short or the margin is thin, you hear it from us first, in words you can act on. Clarity over comfort.

**3. Own the outcome.**
We don't hand off a deliverable and step back. We're accountable to your decision, not just to your statements.

**4. Earn trust in the details.**
A misaligned total, a vague footnote, a late close — small failures break the thing we sell. We sweat the invisible details because that's where confidence lives.

**5. Quietly relentless.**
No theatrics, no jargon, no inflated dashboards. Just consistent, compounding rigor that makes us boring to worry about and impossible to replace.

## 6 · Brand Voice

**Voice in one line:** *A trusted advisor who has read everything, hides nothing, and respects your time.*

### Tone spectrum (where we sit)
```
Formal      ●────────────────○──────  Casual        → confident, not stiff
Technical   ──────────●──────────────  Plain         → precise, then translated
Reserved    ────────────────●────────  Expressive    → calm authority, occasional wit
Cautious    ──────●──────────────────  Bold          → decisive, never reckless
```

### Voice principles
1. **Lead with the answer.** Conclusion first, support second. Busy people are reading.
2. **Translate, don't decorate.** Define the term once, then speak plainly. Never hide behind finance jargon.
3. **Specific beats grand.** "Closed by the 5th" not "best-in-class turnaround." Numbers earn trust; adjectives spend it.
4. **Calm under pressure.** Even when the news is hard, the voice is steady. We are the adult in the room.
5. **Respect the reader's intelligence and clock.** Short sentences. No filler. No hype.

### Lexicon
| We say | We avoid |
| --- | --- |
| partner, counsel, decision, clarity, runway, margin, the close | synergy, leverage (as verb), unleash, revolutionize |
| reconciled, closed, traceable, audit-ready | best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge, next-gen |
| "Here's what it means." | "Robust, scalable, end-to-end solution." |
| your clerk, your team | resources, assets (about people) |

### Voice in practice
- **Hero headline:** "Books that close the month for you." / "Know what your numbers are telling you."
- **Product line:** "Every entry traceable to a human and a source document."
- **Error / empty state:** "We haven't connected this account yet. Link it and your clerk takes it from here."
- **Sales close:** "Twenty minutes to walk your books. A fixed monthly quote. A clerk reconciling by next week."
- **Hard truth, on-brand:** "Your runway is 4.6 months at current burn. Here are the two levers that change that."

## 8 · Typography System

Three tiers: a **refined grotesk** for almost everything (Stripe/Linear lineage), an **editorial serif** reserved for marketing gravitas (McKinsey/Deloitte lineage), and a **monospace** for figures — because finance lives and dies on aligned numbers.

### 8.1 Typefaces
| Role | Primary (licensed) | Open-source fallback (ships now) | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Display & UI | **Söhne** (Klim) | **Inter** | Neutral, precise, exceptional at small sizes and high density |
| Editorial display | **Tiempos Headline** | **Fraunces** | Authority + warmth for hero statements and pull-quotes |
| Monospace / figures | **Söhne Mono** | **Geist Mono** / IBM Plex Mono | Tabular numerals; financial tables must align |

```css
--font-sans: "Söhne", "Inter", system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif;
--font-serif: "Tiempos Headline", "Fraunces", Georgia, serif;
--font-mono: "Söhne Mono", "Geist Mono", "IBM Plex Mono", monospace;
/* Always enable tabular numerals for any financial figure */
.figure { font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums; }
```

> **Rule:** Editorial serif is for *moments* — hero H1, pull-quotes, section openers. Never for UI, never for body, never for data. Default to the grotesk.

### 8.2 Type scale
Base 16px / 1rem. Tuned modular scale (~1.22), with editorial display sizes that break the scale for impact.

| Token | Size (px / rem) | Line height | Weight | Tracking | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `display-xl` | 76 / 4.75 | 1.02 | 400 (serif) / 500 (sans) | -0.02em | Hero statement |
| `display-l` | 56 / 3.5 | 1.05 | 400–500 | -0.02em | Section openers |
| `h1` | 44 / 2.75 | 1.08 | 500 | -0.018em | Page titles |
| `h2` | 34 / 2.125 | 1.12 | 500 | -0.015em | Section heads |
| `h3` | 26 / 1.625 | 1.18 | 500 | -0.01em | Card / block titles |
| `h4` | 20 / 1.25 | 1.3 | 500 | -0.006em | Sub-heads |
| `body-l` | 18 / 1.125 | 1.55 | 400 | 0 | Lead paragraphs |
| `body-m` | 16 / 1 | 1.6 | 400 | 0 | **Base body** |
| `body-s` | 14 / 0.875 | 1.5 | 400 | 0 | Secondary, table cells |
| `caption` | 13 / 0.8125 | 1.4 | 400 | 0 | Captions, helper text |
| `overline` | 12 / 0.75 | 1.3 | 500 | +0.12em ·UPPERCASE | Eyebrows, labels |
| `figure-xl` | 40 / 2.5 | 1.0 | 500 ·mono ·tabular | -0.01em | KPI hero numbers |

### 8.3 Responsive display (fluid)
```css
--display-xl: clamp(2.9rem, 6.8vw, 4.75rem);
--display-l:  clamp(2.2rem, 4.6vw, 3.5rem);
--h1:         clamp(2rem, 3.6vw, 2.75rem);
--h2:         clamp(1.6rem, 2.6vw, 2.125rem);
```

### 8.4 Rules
- Headlines read in **5–10 strong words**. No six-line headings.
- Body measure: **60–75 characters** max (`max-width: 68ch`).
- Never use more than **two weights** of the grotesk on one screen (400 + 500). Bold (600) is a rare emphasis.
- Tracking tightens as size grows; never letterspace lowercase body text.
- All currency, percentages, dates, and table figures use `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`.

## 9 · Button Styles

Buttons must feel **responsive to press** — instant feedback is the whole point. Pill geometry, generous hit area, motion under 160ms.

### 9.1 Variants
| Variant | Background | Text | Border | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary** | `--evergreen-500` | `--canvas` | none | The one action that matters per view |
| **Secondary** | transparent | `--neutral-900` | `1px --neutral-200` | Supporting action |
| **Ghost** | transparent | `--neutral-700` | none | Tertiary / low-emphasis |
| **Link / arrow** | none | `--evergreen-600` | underline-on-hover | Inline navigation |
| **Destructive** | transparent | `--error` | `1px` error @ 30% | Delete, disconnect |
| **On-dark primary** | `--evergreen-500` | `--canvas` | none | Same, over ink surfaces |
| **On-dark ghost** | transparent | `#F3EFE5` | `1px rgba(243,239,229,.13)` | Secondary on dark |

### 9.2 Sizes
| Size | Height | Padding-X | Font | Radius |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| sm | 36px | 14px | 14 / 500 | 100px |
| md (default) | 44px | 22px | 15 / 500 | 100px |
| lg | 52px | 28px | 16 / 500 | 100px |

### 9.3 States
- **Hover** (`@media (hover:hover)` only): primary → `--evergreen-600`; secondary → border darkens to `rgba(20,22,15,.28)`.
- **Active / press:** `transform: scale(0.97)` — universal, every variant.
- **Focus-visible:** `2px` evergreen ring, `2px` offset.
- **Disabled:** `--neutral-100` bg, `--neutral-300` text, no shadow, `cursor: not-allowed`.
- **Loading:** label fades to 0.6, a `14px` spinner replaces the trailing icon; width is locked to prevent reflow.

### 9.4 Reference CSS
```css
.btn {
  display: inline-flex; align-items: center; justify-content: center; gap: .55em;
  height: 44px; padding-inline: 22px; border-radius: 100px;
  font: 500 15px/1 var(--font-sans); letter-spacing: -0.01em;
  transition: transform 150ms var(--ease-out),
              background-color 200ms ease, border-color 200ms ease, color 200ms ease;
  will-change: transform;
}
.btn:active { transform: scale(0.97); }
.btn:focus-visible { outline: 2px solid var(--evergreen-500); outline-offset: 2px; }

.btn--primary { background: var(--evergreen-500); color: var(--canvas); }
.btn--secondary { background: transparent; color: var(--neutral-900); border: 1px solid var(--neutral-200); }
.btn--ghost { background: transparent; color: var(--neutral-700); }

@media (hover: hover) and (pointer: fine) {
  .btn--primary:hover { background: var(--evergreen-600); }
  .btn--secondary:hover { border-color: rgba(20,22,15,.28); }
}
@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) { .btn:active { transform: none; } }
```

Cards lean **flat with hairline borders** (Linear/Stripe), using shadow only to signal genuine elevation (menus, modals, dragged items). Depth is earned, not decorative.

| Variant | Surface | Border | Shadow | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

| **Raised** | `#FFFFFF` | `1px --neutral-100` | `--e2` | Pricing, featured modules |

- Radius: `--radius-lg (20px)` for content cards, `--radius-md (14px)` for compact/data cards.

## 11 · Icon Style

**Linear-grade geometric line icons.** Consistent, calm, precise — they should read as one family with the grotesk.

- **Grid:** 24×24 px artboard, 2px live-area padding (20px content).
- **Stroke:** 1.5px, uniform weight, no tapering.
- **Caps & joins:** round (`stroke-linecap: round`, `stroke-linejoin: round`).
- **Corners:** 2px radius on geometric shapes — softened, not sharp, never fully rounded.
- **Fill:** outline by default; solid fill reserved for active/selected nav states only.
- **Color:** `currentColor` always — icons inherit text color, accent only when interactive.
- **Optical sizing:** provide 16 / 20 / 24 masters; don't scale 24px down to 16px (strokes get muddy).

**Recommended base set:** Lucide or Untitled UI, restyled to 1.5px for consistency. Custom-draw finance-specific glyphs (ledger, reconcile, runway, close) to match.

**Do / Don't**
| Do | Don't |
| --- | --- |
| Uniform 1.5px stroke | Mixed weights in one set |
| `currentColor` inheritance | Multicolor or gradient icons |
| Round caps, 2px corners | Sharp corners + flat caps mixed |
| Solid fill for active nav only | Decorative filled icons everywhere |

## 12 · Illustration Style

**Data as art — never clip-art.** We don't draw cartoon people juggling coins. We render the *texture of finance*: precision, structure, flow.

### Approach
- **Abstract systems & topographies:** isometric data planes, contour lines reading like financial terrain, fine grid fields, ledger-rule lines that become composition.
- **Material honesty:** paper grain, hairline rules, brass foil accents — restrained, editorial.
- **Generative line work:** sparklines, recon bars, balance flows abstracted into elegant geometry.
- **Real interface fragments:** crops of the actual product (dashboards, recon rows) used as hero imagery — proof, not decoration.

### Rules
- Palette-locked: evergreen + graphite on canvas, brass as the single highlight. No off-brand color.
- Line weight echoes the icon system (1.5px) for family resemblance.
- Geometry over whimsy; tension and negative space over busyness.
- One concept per illustration — it should be readable in a glance.

### Photography direction (when humans appear)
Editorial, desaturated, serious-but-warm. Real operators in real workspaces — not stock "diverse team high-fiving." Natural light, shallow depth, graded toward neutral-warm. People are *clients we respect*, never props.

**Banned:** 3D blobs, floating orbs, gradient mascots, hand-drawn doodles, stock-photo handshakes, fake glowing dashboards.

## 13 · Animation Guidelines

Motion exists to **explain, confirm, and prevent jarring change** — never to entertain. Restraint reads as premium. (Aligned to Emil Kowalski's design-engineering principles.)

### 13.1 The decision framework
1. **Should it animate?** How often will the user see it?
   - 100×/day (keyboard actions, command palette) → **no animation, ever.**
   - 10×/day (hover, list nav) → minimal or none.
   - Occasional (modals, drawers, toasts) → standard.
   - Rare / first-time (onboarding, success) → can add delight.
2. **What's the purpose?** State change, spatial continuity, feedback, or preventing a jarring jump. If the answer is "it looks cool" and it's seen often — don't.
3. **Easing?** Entering/exiting → `ease-out`. Moving/morphing on screen → `ease-in-out`. Hover/color → `ease`. Constant (marquee, progress) → `linear`. **Never `ease-in` for UI.**
4. **How fast?** Under 300ms for UI. Faster *feels* more responsive.

### 13.2 Tokens
```css
--ease-out:    cubic-bezier(0.23, 1, 0.32, 1);   /* UI enter/exit — strong */
--ease-in-out: cubic-bezier(0.77, 0, 0.175, 1);  /* on-screen movement */
--ease-std:    cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1);      /* utility */
--spring:      { type: spring, duration: 0.5, bounce: 0.2 }; /* drag / playful */

--dur-press: 150ms;   /* button press */
--dur-fast:  120ms;   /* tooltips, small popovers */
--dur-base:  220ms;   /* dropdowns, cards */
--dur-mod:   280ms;   /* selects */
--dur-slow:  420ms;   /* modals, drawers */
```

### 13.3 Duration by element
| Element | Duration |
| --- | --- |
| Button press feedback | 120–160ms |
| Tooltip / small popover | 125–200ms |
| Dropdown / select | 180–250ms |
| Modal / drawer | 280–420ms |
| Scroll reveal | 600–720ms (decorative, generous) |
| Marketing / explanatory | longer permitted |

### 13.4 Patterns
- **Enter:** `opacity 0 → 1` + `scale(0.985) → 1` + `translateY(16px) → 0`. **Never `scale(0)`** — nothing appears from nothing.
- **Press:** `scale(0.97)` on every pressable.
- **Popover origin:** scale from the trigger (`transform-origin` = trigger), not center. Modals stay centered.
- **Stagger:** 30–80ms between list items; never block interaction.
- **Interruptible UI** (toasts, toggles): CSS *transitions*, not keyframes — transitions retarget mid-flight.
- **Performance:** animate only `transform` and `opacity` (GPU). Never `width`, `height`, `margin`, `top`.

### 13.5 Accessibility
```css
@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) {
  *, *::before, *::after {
    animation-duration: .01ms !important;
    transition-duration: .01ms !important;
  }
  /* Keep opacity/color fades that aid comprehension; remove all movement. */
}
```
- Gate every hover transform behind `@media (hover: hover) and (pointer: fine)`.

`--maxw-content: 1200px; --maxw-prose: 68ch;`

### 14.5 Component inventory
- **Forms:** inputs 44px tall, `--radius-md`, `1px --neutral-200` border → evergreen on focus + ring; floating helper/error text in `caption`; never use placeholder as label.
- **Navigation:** sticky top bar 76px, transparent over dark hero → glass `blur(14px)` + hairline on scroll. App: 240px left rail, collapsible to 64px icon-only.
- **Tables (finance-critical):** right-align all numerics, `tabular-nums`, zebra via `--neutral-50` on hover not stripes, sticky header, hairline rows.
- **Data viz:** evergreen primary, neutral comparison, brass for the single highlighted series; thin axes, no gridline clutter; always label the latest value inline.
- **Chips / tags:** `--evergreen-50` bg, `--evergreen-700` text, `--radius-full`, 12px overline.
- **States:** every component ships default / hover / focus / active / disabled / loading / empty / error.
- **Empty states:** one line of plain copy + one primary action. Never a dead end.
- **Toasts:** enter & exit from the same edge (bottom-right), swipe-to-dismiss, CSS transitions.

### 16.3 Mobile rules
- **Navigation:** hamburger → full-screen overlay menu (marketing); bottom tab bar, 5 items max (app).
- **Sticky CTA:** primary action docks to the bottom once the hero scrolls away — never covering content.
- **Reorder, don't shrink:** on mobile, lead with the copy or the visual deliberately (e.g., dashboard above its caption) rather than cramming a desktop split.
- **Tables → cards:** financial tables reflow into stacked key/value rows; keep figures right-aligned and `tabular-nums`.
- **Type:** body never below 16px (prevents iOS zoom-on-focus); display uses the fluid `clamp()` floors.
- **Gestures:** swipe-to-dismiss toasts and sheets; momentum-based (velocity > 0.11 dismisses); damping at boundaries.
